Quick Facts

  • Capital: Abuja
  • Official Name: Federal Republic of Nigeria
  • Region: West Africa (Africa)
  • Population: Over 220 million
  • Area: ~923,768 sq km

Geography

Nigeria is located in West Africa and borders Benin, Niger, Chad, and Cameroon, with a southern coastline along the Gulf of Guinea. The country has diverse landscapes including coastal plains, tropical rainforests, savannahs, and semi-arid regions in the north. The climate varies from equatorial in the south to arid in the north.

People and Society

Nigeria is the most populous country in Africa and one of the most culturally diverse in the world, with over 250 ethnic groups. Major languages include English (official), Hausa, Yoruba, and Igbo. Religions are mainly Christianity and Islam, with traditional beliefs also present.

Economy

Nigeria has the largest economy in Africa by GDP. Its economy is driven by oil and gas exports, agriculture, telecommunications, and services. The country is one of the world’s major oil producers, though efforts are being made to diversify into technology, manufacturing, and finance.
