Country Profile

Quick Facts

  • Capital: Victoria
  • Official Name: Republic of Seychelles
  • Region: Africa (Indian Ocean)
  • Population: ~100,000
  • Area: ~459 sq km

Geography

Seychelles is an island country in the Indian Ocean, northeast of Madagascar. It consists of an archipelago of many islands, with mountainous inner islands and low-lying coral islands. The climate is tropical, generally warm and humid, influenced by the sea.

People and Society

The population is small and concentrated mainly on Mahé, Praslin, and La Digue. Seychellois Creole, English, and French are widely used. The society reflects a blend of African, European, and Asian influences, with Christianity being the dominant religion alongside other faiths.

Economy

Seychelles has a small, tourism-driven economy supported by fishing and related industries. Tourism is the leading sector, while tuna fishing and fish processing are important sources of export earnings. Like many island economies, Seychelles is sensitive to global travel trends and external economic shocks.
